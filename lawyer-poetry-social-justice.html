Why This Lawyer Writes Poetry

📅 May 14, 2026
✎ Paola Vargas
⌚ 5 min read

Most of Paola Vargas's writing lives in court filings and academic journals. Some of it lives in a poetry book — and she'll tell you the two aren't as different as they sound.

Legal writing and academic research both demand precision, but they also demand restraint. There's a version of every case, every study, that doesn't make it onto the page — the version with grief in it, or anger, or the specific texture of what it actually feels like to be the person the paperwork is about. Paola started writing poetry, in part, because that version of the story needed somewhere to go.

A Different Kind of Precision

She describes creative writing as an "excellent medium" for a passion for social justice that doesn't always fit inside a legal brief or a peer-reviewed article. Where legal writing has to prove a claim beyond argument, poetry can sit with a feeling long enough to actually understand it — before, sometimes, that understanding finds its way back into her advocacy work anyway.

It's not a hobby she keeps separate from the rest of her career so much as another instrument in the same toolkit. The research years taught her to find the pattern in the data. The law taught her to build an argument from that pattern. The poetry is where she lets herself sit with what the pattern actually means for the people living inside it.

Creative writing is an excellent medium for a relentless passion for all things social justice.

Writing as an Extension of Advocacy

Paola's path — public health researcher, then first-generation law student, then attorney — is already an unusual one. Add "published poet" and it starts to look less like a series of separate chapters and more like one long argument for paying closer attention: to data, to people, to the words used to describe both.

That same throughline runs through her research-to-law career path, where the connecting thread was always the same question: who is being left out, and how do you make sure they're seen? Poetry, for her, is simply another way of asking it.

Where to Find Her Writing

Paola shares readings and updates on her creative work through Vimeo and posts day-to-day glimpses of the writing life — mountains, coffee, and all — on Instagram.

She's a first-generation Latina, a mother, an attorney, and a writer, in whichever order makes sense on a given day. Read more about her full story on the About page.

Conclusion

Paola Vargas didn't set out to become a poet any more than she set out to become an attorney. Both grew out of the same relentless instinct: to notice what usually goes unsaid, and to insist that it's written down anyway.
